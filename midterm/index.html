Can music help a machine have feelings?

The album explores the themes of human connections and technology. Its name comes from the idea of music and songs as information that we receive, "data" that shapes the way we are. In response to that, Tainy envisioned the album as a film about a cyborg named Sena who comes to life through songs. In DATA, Tainy creates a cyborg-human hybrid named Sena 瀬奈, with the goal of making her as close to a human being as possible. He strives to achive this by uploading human emotions and memories through music. Music, in many ways represents the meaning of life; all the emotions and memories we humans feel and create during our lifetime. Each song on the album represents a feeling or a memory that Sena experiences. Through Sena´s upload process, we witness her transformation and can reflect on what it means to be “human”.

Facts about DATA

  • DATA is Tainy’s first solo studio album, and it reportedly took about three years to complete, with early ideas starting after a trip to Tulum, Mexico.
  • Most of the album was recorded in Miami, but sessions also took place in Puerto Rico, Mexico, Spain, New York, Los Angeles, and Kyoto, reflecting the global feel of the project
  • Tainy drew heavily from Japanese anime and science fiction to shape DATA’s futuristic aesthetic and “film-like” narrative world, something he has mentioned in interviews about the project.
  • DATA functions almost like a curated universe of contemporary reggaeton: it features stars like Bad Bunny, Rauw Alejandro, Daddy Yankee, Feid, Myke Towers, Arcángel, Wisin & Yandel, Mora, Young Miko, Ozuna, and more, plus EDM names like Skrillex and Four Tet.
